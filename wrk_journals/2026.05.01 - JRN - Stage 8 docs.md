## Why this fell out cleanly

Stage 7's submodule-level demotions (27 submodules to `pub(crate)`) made
all their internal `pub` items unreachable from outside the crate. Items
that *are* still public are either:

- Re-exported through the prelude or top-level `pub use` lines —
  re-exports inherit the original item's docs, and those originals were
  already documented as part of the documented public API.
- Top-level types in modules that were already kept `pub` for documented
  consumer access (`rtp::RtpSession`, `srtp::SrtpContext`, etc.), which
  had docs throughout because they were part of the original
  documented public API across the former workspace crates.

In other words: the crate already had good doc hygiene on its
**intentional** public surface. The reason `#![deny(missing_docs)]` was
demoted in Stage 1 was that the *unintentional* surface (everything
crossing former-crate boundaries) was undocumented. Stage 7 shrank that
unintentional surface to nothing, so `deny` slots back in for free.

This is the cleanest possible outcome for an item that the HLD explicitly
flagged as "could be 20 items or 200; we won't know until cargo doc runs."
The answer turned out to be zero.
